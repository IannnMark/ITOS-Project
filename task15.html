Internet of Things (IoT)


The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to the network of physical objects, devices, vehicles, buildings, and other items that are embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies to enable them to connect and exchange data with each other and with cloud-based applications and services. IoT has the potential to transform industries and improve people's lives by enabling new applications and services that were previously impossible.


IoT platform technologies


  • Connectivity platforms
  • Cloud platforms
  • Application enablement platforms (AEPs)

Connectivity platform


These platforms provide the infrastructure and protocols needed to connect IoT devices to each other and to the internet. Examples include Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Zigbee, and cellular networks.


Cloud Platforms


These platforms provide the infrastructure and services needed to process and store the large amounts of data generated by IoT devices.


Application enablement platforms


These platforms provide tools for developing and deploying IoT applications and services, such as device management, data analytics, and application development frameworks.
